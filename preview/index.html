Introduction to Thomistic Philosophy

Module Info

This module introduces students to the foundational concepts of Thomistic philosophy, exploring the synthesis of faith and reason developed by St. Thomas Aquinas. We will examine key texts from the Summa Theologiae and discuss their relevance to contemporary theological discourse.

Instructor: Dr. Sarah Johnson

Duration: 8 weeks

Reflection Question

How does Aquinas's understanding of the relationship between faith and reason challenge or affirm your own approach to theological study? Provide specific examples from this week's readings.

Your Response

Discussion (3 Responses)

Maria Garcia Dec 9, 2024 at 3:42 PM

Aquinas's synthesis has been transformative for my understanding. I was raised with the impression that faith and reason were somehow opposed, but reading Question 1 of the Summa opened my eyes to how they can work together harmoniously. What struck me most was his assertion that sacred doctrine uses reason in service of faith, not as its foundation. This seems to address many of the concerns raised in Fides et Ratio about rationalism.

Fr. Michael Brown Dec 9, 2024 at 4:15 PM

Excellent observation, Maria! You've touched on something central to Thomistic epistemology. The distinction between reason "in service of" vs. "as foundation of" faith is crucial.

James Chen Dec 9, 2024 at 5:30 PM

Maria, I had a similar experience growing up. The either/or mentality seems to be a modern phenomenon that Aquinas's work helps us overcome.

Susan Williams Dec 9, 2024 at 6:22 PM

I appreciate the readings, but I'm still wrestling with some questions. If faith goes beyond reason, how do we determine when we've crossed that threshold? Aquinas seems confident about the boundary, but in practice it feels less clear.
